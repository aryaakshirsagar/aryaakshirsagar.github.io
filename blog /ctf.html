CTF Tips from a Fellow Player

Not an expert here, just someone who’s been down the rabbit hole of CTFs at 2 AM. These are some notes I wish I had when I started.

  1. Read the Question Slowly

    First mistake I used to make: rushing. Take a breath, read it twice. Sometimes the challenge literally tells you the answer if you slow down.

  2. Keep a Scratchpad

    Open up a Notion page, text file, or even pen & paper. Jot down commands, hints, random strings you find. Your future self will thank you.

  3. Small Wins > Staring at One Challenge Forever

    Stuck for more than 20–30 mins? Park it. Go get some easy flags to keep morale up. You can always come back with a fresh brain.

  4. Learn the Basics (Really)

    A lot of CTF challenges are just “basic” commands in disguise. Stuff like strings, grep, file, base64 decoding, and simple scripting solves more challenges than you think.

  5. Learn the Common Tools

    A few tools will get you very far. Here are some must-haves:

    • strings: Pull out readable text from binaries.
    • binwalk: Great for picking apart firmware or files.
    • john / hashcat: Password cracking tools.
    • exiftool: Dig into file metadata.
    Bonus: Have cyberchef bookmarked. It’s like a Swiss Army knife for encoding/decoding and random transformations.

  6. Don’t Ignore “Weird” Files

    If there’s a random text file or image in the challenge folder, open it. Look at it in hex, open it in a text editor, check its metadata — anything. Sometimes the flag is hiding in plain sight.

  7. Play with Friends

    CTFs are way more fun in a team. Plus, you’ll learn way faster when you see how others approach problems.

  8. Practice Off-CTF

    Platforms like TryHackMe, HackTheBox, or PicoCTF are great for keeping your skills sharp even when there’s no live CTF going on.

Good luck, and may your flags be real and your hints be kind.
